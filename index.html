Unbiased Sequential Recommendation with Latent Confounders

Anonymous Author  

1. Abstract

Sequential recommendation holds the promise of understanding user preference by capturing successive behavior correlations. Existing research essentially focus on designing different models for better fitting the offline datasets. However, the observational data may have been contaminated by the exposure or selection biases, which renders the learned sequential models unreliable. In order to solve this fundamental problem, in this paper, we propose to reformulate the sequential recommendation task with the potential outcome framework, where we are able to clearly understand the data bias mechanism and correct it by re-weighting the training instances with the inverse propensity score (IPS). For more robustness modeling, a clipping strategy is applied to the IPS estimation to reduce the variance of the learning objective. To make our framework more practical, we design a parameterized model to remove the impact of the potential latent confounders. At last, we theoretically analysis the unbiasedness of the proposed framework under both vanilla and clipping IPS estimations. We conduct extensive experiment based on both synthetic and real-world datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of our framework.

Contributions

  • We propose to solve the sequential recommendation task within the potential outcome framework for data bias correction, which, to the best of our knowledge, is the first time in the recommendation domain.
  • To realize the above idea, we design a three-channel GRU model to simultaneously capture the item correlations, model the latent confounders and estimate the IPS.
  • We analyze the unbiasedness of the proposed model from both expectation and variance perspectives, which builds the theoretical foundations of our model.
  • We conduct extensive experiments based on both synthetic and real-world datasets to evaluate our proposed models.

5. Usage

5.1. Download the code

5.2. Download the dataset, and put it into data/dataset name/ folder.

5.3. run main.py file.

For example: python main.py --dataset mind --batch_size 64 --lr 0.01 --embed_dim 64 --clip 0.

where lr denotes learning rate and clip denotes clipping strategy, more specifically, when we set clip == 0, that means without clipping strategy.
